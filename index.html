The Eduba frame

Sixty. Thirty. Ten.

Our working model on any stack is 60/30/10. Sixty percent of what looks like AI work is traditional code and database work. Thirty percent is rule-based logic that does not need a model. Ten percent is genuine AI, and that is where the budget has to stretch.

Allocation
60% code and database 30% rule-based logic 10% genuine AI

Applied to Fluentis the split is concrete. Partner enablement (documentation generation, release-note drafting, demo environment provisioning) is mostly code and workflow. Anomaly detection inside AP, financial close, and inventory variance is the rule-based layer with a narrow ML seam. Conversational query and document extraction from supplier invoices are the honest AI problems. A clean layering here gives the product team a release plan and gives the partner channel content that does not require a Sacile engineer on every onboarding call.

Case

Feeld. A CTO who wanted AI to land without a rewrite.

Feeld is a product company. The CTO wanted AI to land inside an existing application without a rewrite and without burning out the team. The engagement was a scoped sprint: one workshop, advisory calls, an Organizational Context Architecture, and a Strategic Operations Framework the product team could keep running against. The pattern is the one Fluentis needs. A product leader sets the shape. The team executes against a context structure they already understand.

Paper

The methodology is published.

Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS. Agent context organized as a layered filesystem so a product team can extend capability without a rewrite, and partners can extend the platform without touching the core. Repository: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-.
